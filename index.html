The Problem

The market is crowded.
And broken.

Every top app owns one lane. None of them talk to each other. Users end up managing a fragmented stack — and quitting when the guilt kicks in.

01
Fragmentation
Strava tracks your run. MyFitnessPal logs your food. WHOOP monitors your sleep. None of this data connects. You're left to make sense of it alone.
02
Shame mechanics
Missed a streak? Logged too many calories? Apps punish gaps instead of building resilience. Research shows this triggers guilt, avoidance, and dropout.
03
No real AI
Most "AI coaches" are motivational quotes on a timer. They don't know you're under-fuelled, under-slept, or overdue for a rest day. FitFlow does.
04
Decision fatigue
Apps present libraries. They don't answer the only question that matters every morning: what should I actually do today?
